Overview

Eburet is an online furniture store that needed a full redesign — from information architecture to visual identity. The goal was to create a premium, intuitive shopping experience that reflects the quality and aesthetic of the brand.

The project covers end-to-end UX research, wireframing, UI design, and a working prototype — all delivered as a comprehensive case study.

Challenge

The original store suffered from an overcrowded layout, poor navigation hierarchy, and inconsistent visual language. Users struggled to find products and the checkout flow had high drop-off rates.

My task was to redesign the full experience — balancing aesthetic ambition with conversion-focused UX decisions, and maintaining the brand's premium positioning.

Design Process

Starting with user research and competitor analysis, I mapped out the key pain points in the existing experience. Card sorting sessions revealed that users expected product categories to be organised differently than the current taxonomy.

The new information architecture was built around how customers actually think about furniture — by room, by style, and by material — rather than by internal product codes. This single change reduced average navigation depth by two steps.

Visual Solution

The visual system draws from Scandinavian minimalism — generous white space, a restrained colour palette of warm neutrals and deep forest tones, and typography that feels editorial without being cold.

Product photography is given maximum real estate. The grid system was designed to let the furniture breathe, letting customers form an emotional connection with each piece before clicking through.
