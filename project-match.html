Overview

Match is a multidisciplinary project combining graphic design, 3D art direction, and AI-generated visuals. The work explores the intersection of human emotion and digital aesthetics — creating a visual language that feels simultaneously organic and computational.

Each image is a deliberate composition — the result of iterative art direction, material exploration, and the interplay between intention and generative surprise.

Concept

The project takes its name from the idea of unexpected pairings — materials that don't belong together, colours that clash beautifully, forms that echo across vastly different scales.

Textures drawn from organic nature — coral, moss, crystal, skin — are placed in direct tension with synthetic surfaces, digital artefacts, and mechanical precision.

Art Direction

Each frame was art directed with a specific emotional register in mind. The colour palette shifts across the series — from cool, mineralogical blues and whites to saturated greens and pinks that feel almost biological.

Depth of field, lighting temperature, and surface reflectivity were controlled to give each piece a distinct mood while keeping the series visually coherent.

Process

The workflow combined traditional 3D rendering with AI image refinement — using generative tools not as a shortcut, but as a collaborator. Prompts were iterated extensively, and the most interesting results came from deliberately ambiguous inputs that produced unexpected textures and forms.

Post-processing added the final layer of colour grading and tonal consistency that ties the series together as a cohesive body of work.
